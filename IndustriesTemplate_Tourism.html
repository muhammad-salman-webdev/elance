DEI status in the Tourism Industry

The tourism industry, with its diverse workforce, is poised to lead by example. Although it has made progress in diversity at entry and mid-level roles, leadership diversity lags. Influenced by Gen-Z, who value their employer's social impact, the industry adapts through inclusive practices to address high turnover rates and enhance equity.

Challenges in the tourism industry

  • Leadership Diversity Gap:

    A significant underrepresentation of minority groups in executive positions, hindering a full spectrum of perspectives at decision-making levels.

  • High turnover rates and Gen Z:

    Gen-Z's presence in the tourism workforce drives a transformative shift toward more inclusive and equitable workplace practices challenging the industry to reduce turnover by aligning with their values and aspirations.

  • Cultural Competence:

    The industry's vast interface with global cultures demands a workforce well-versed in cultural sensitivity and inclusivity, yet many organizations fall short.

  • Accessibility and Inclusion:

    Ensuring that travel services and experiences are accessible and welcoming to people of all abilities remains an area for improvement.
